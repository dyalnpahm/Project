Queensland

Queensland is a beautiful state in the northeastern part of Australia. It's known for its stunning beaches, the Great Barrier Reef, and its abundance of natural beauty. Whether you're looking to relax on the beach, go hiking in the rainforest, or explore the underwater world, Queensland has something for everyone.

Explore the Great Barrier Reef

The Great Barrier Reef is the world's largest coral reef system and it's located off the coast of Queensland. It's home to an incredible diversity of marine life and it's a must-see for anyone visiting the state. There are many different ways to explore the reef, such as snorkeling, diving, or taking a boat tour.

Hike Through the Daintree Rainforest

The Daintree Rainforest is the oldest living rainforest in the world, and it's located in the far north of Queensland. It's home to an incredible diversity of plant and animal life, including many species that are found nowhere else on Earth. There are several hiking trails that wind through the rainforest, allowing you to get up close and personal with this unique ecosystem.

Sail Through the Whitsunday Islands

The Whitsunday Islands are a group of 74 tropical islands located off the coast of Queensland. They're known for their crystal clear waters, white sandy beaches, and picturesque landscapes. You can take a sailing trip through the islands and stop at secluded beaches along the way.

Explore Fraser Island

Fraser Island is the largest sand island in the world and it's located off the coast of Queensland. It's home to an incredible diversity of plant and animal life, including the rare Fraser Island dingo. There are many hiking trails, swimming holes, and other outdoor activities to enjoy on the island.
